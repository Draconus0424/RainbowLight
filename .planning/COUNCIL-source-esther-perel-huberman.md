## Core Philosophies

### 1. Identity and Change Are Both the Point

"We meet other in order to find ourselves and we meet an other and want to be surprised by the self we haven't known."

"What draws me to you in the beginning because it is different may expand me. But it's also the very thing that becomes the source of conflict later because we want to change but up to a point, not too much, and not on your terms."

### 2. The Core Task of Every Relationship

"How do I get close to you without losing me? And how do I hold on to me without losing you?"

"One person more afraid of losing the other, one person more afraid of losing themselves. One person more afraid with the fear of abandonment and one person more afraid with the fear of suffocation."

### 3. Curiosity vs Reactivity

"Curiosity stands in opposition to reactivity. Reactivity reinforces the cycle -- narrow repetition, rapid cycles of escalation, defense and attack. Curiosity is an active engagement with the unknown."

### 4. Stories Are Not Facts

"The story shapes the experience. When people hold on to the story and they don't think it's a story, they think it's fact. 'This is what happened last night. I'll tell you what you did.' That's not the case. It's a subjective rendering."

### 5. Love and Desire Are Separate Systems

"Love and desire -- they relate but they also conflict. They're not one and the same. They don't need the same things. They don't thrive on the same elements."

"Tell me how you were loved and I will tell you how you make love. Your emotional history is inscribed in the physicality of sex."

### 6. The Erotic Is About Aliveness, Not Sex

"The erotic is not the sexual. The erotic is the quality of aliveness, vibrancy, vitality, hopefulness, curiosity, imagination, playfulness."

"Affairs -- people say it's not that I wanted to find another person, it's that I wanted to find another self."

### 7. Repair Is Not Enough -- Revival Is the Goal

"Repair is not the end of the story. The revival is the end of the story."

Three stages: (1) Acknowledgment with genuine remorse for the IMPACT, not just the act. (2) Vigilance -- becoming the protector of the relationship, showing the other person matters in detail. (3) Erotic recovery -- new, risky, curious experiences that create a new "us."

### 8. Self-Esteem Is Seeing Your Flaws and Still Respecting Yourself

"Self-esteem is your ability to see yourself as a flawed individual and still hold yourself in high regard. When you admit your flaw it means there's something wrong with you -- then it's very hard to say I'm sorry."

### 9. Confirmation Bias and Fundamental Attribution Error

"Confirmation bias -- I'm looking for evidence that reinforces my beliefs and I disregard any evidence that contradicts it."

"Fundamental attribution error -- if I'm in a bad mood it's because of circumstances. If you're in a bad mood it's because you're a contentious person."

### 10. The First Question

"Knowing yourself as well as you do, what do you think makes it hard to live with you?"
